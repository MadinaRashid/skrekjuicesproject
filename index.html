Frequently Asked Questions

~Which vegetable is used for juice?

Just like cucumber, celery has a 95% water content which makes it great for juicing and a good base. These vegetables may look unappealing, but they actually contain very high amounts of vitamin K, A, C and folate. Celery leaves also contain high amounts of potassium.

~What is the function of vegetable juice?

Juicing can play a role in a balanced diet. For instance, it can help you get fruits and vegetables if you don't enjoy eating them. Juicing also can give the digestive system a rest from digesting fiber. This may help some people who have certain health conditions or who receive certain medical treatments.

~What is the process of vegetable juice?

Vegetable juices are essentially disintegrated vegetables, thus separating juice as a fluid from the solids by means of mechanical devices. They are constituted from cellular juice and a part of crushed pulp, from the tissues of some vegetables and comprise vitamins, sugars, acids, mineral salts, pectic substances etc.

~Can we drink vegetable juice daily?

Drinking green juice in moderation can boost your intake of multiple nutrients, but too much may cause serious side effects. Green vegetables are a rich source of oxalic acid, or oxalate, which is considered an antinutrient because it binds to minerals in food and stops your digestive tract from absorbing them.
